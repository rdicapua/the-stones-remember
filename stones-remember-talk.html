The Talk

Three lives. One palazzo.
One impossible century.

In this nonfiction talk, Madelena Kishner and Riccardo Di Capua explore the intertwined lives of three of the twentieth century's most extraordinary figures — set against the stones, water, and light of Venice.

They focus on 1948: the year Peggy Guggenheim established her modern art collection in the same crumbling palazzo where Luisa Casati had once staged an avant-garde life so extravagant it beggared a fortune and astonished a continent.

At the center of both women's stories stands Gabriele D'Annunzio — poet, seducer, proto-fascist, and the intellectual catalyst whose radical ideas about beauty and morality set the century on fire, and shaped the lives of both women.

About the Presenters

Bound by curiosity.
Driven by craft.

Williams Island, Florida

Madelena Kishner and Riccardo Di Capua are colleagues bound by a shared love of creativity, curiosity, and the kind of give-and-take intellectual adventure that produces something neither could make alone.

Their books range from the Venetian decadence of The Stones Remember — tracing the intertwined fates of Peggy Guggenheim, Luisa Casati, and Gabriele D'Annunzio — to the global sweep of Rooms Where History Broke, fifteen narrative nonfiction stories of the moments when hidden forces changed everything; from the accessible wonder of Materials: The Spectacular Science of Stuff to their most recent work, Artificial Intelligence in Our Daily Lives, which makes the case that the future everyone is waiting for has already arrived.

Kishner brings to the partnership a background in fintech and a gift for atmospheric, character-driven prose that renders historical figures with psychological intimacy. Di Capua brings analytical rigor and a materials scientist's eye for structure, innovation, and the beauty that comes from within.

Together they have lectured and presented on topics spanning the history of science, the nature of artistic obsession, the biography of materials, and the cultural impact of artificial intelligence. Their podcast series — including Rooms Where History Broke, Whispers of the Unseen, and Journey Through Time — extend their narrative work into audio, reaching listeners who want history told the way it actually felt to live it: urgent, strange, and impossible to put down.
